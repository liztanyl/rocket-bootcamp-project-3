Game play

  • Who Dis? is played in 3 rounds.
  • In each round, a person from the starting team has 60 seconds to get their team to guess as many names as possible from the deck by giving clues about the person's identity.
  • Teams and players take turns giving clues.
  • There's no limit to the number of guesses.
  • Skipping is allowed and highly encouraged. Skipped cards are shuffled into the deck for continued gameplay.
  • A round ends when all cards from the deck have been guessed correctly. Scores are tallied and the deck is shuffled.

Game Round

Each of the 3 rounds has a restriction on how you can give clues:

  • Round 1: Use any word, sound or gesture. You can't use the name itself. If you use any part of the name, you have to skip that card. Reading the clue text is allowed.
  • Round 2: Use only 1 word as a clue. It can be anything except the name itself. You can repeat that word as much as you like, but no sounds or gestures.
  • Round 3: Act out the clues. No words. Sound effects are OK (within reason).
